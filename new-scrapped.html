In a bizarre incident from Karimnagar district, Telangana, traffic police intercepted a motorcyclist during a routine vehicle check at Sikhwadi and were stunned to find that he had accumulated a staggering 277 pending challans. The total penalty amounted to ₹79,845 for repeated violations—mostly for riding without a helmet. The biker, identified as Abdul Kayyum, had been ignoring the fines for months until the police seized his vehicle. Authorities have urged citizens to clear pending challans promptly and adhere to traffic safety rules to avoid such extreme cases.
